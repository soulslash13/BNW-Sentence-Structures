Summary of Compound Sentences

A compound sentence is constructed from two independent, and those clauses are joined by a conjunction.

Past Perfect Tense Examples

“Behind them, in the west, the crimson and orange were almost faded; a dark bank of cloud had crept into the zenith” (Huxley 75).

“It was at this moment that Bernard had made his announcement; the Savage wasn’t coming to the party” (174).

Past Tense Examples

“Block above block, each story smaller than the one below, the tall houses rose like stepped and amputated pyramids into the blue sky” (107).

“At their feet lay a straggle of low buildings, a criss-cross of walls; and on three sides the precipices fell sheer into the plain” (107).
